About Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board (TNPCB)

The Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board (TNPCB), established on February 27, 1982, is dedicated to safeguarding the environment through stringent policies and regulations. With a vision to ensure sustainable development, the TNPCB monitors industries and promotes eco-friendly practices.

Core Legislations and Rules

  • The Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974
  • The Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981
  • The Environment (Protection) Act, 1986
  • The Hazardous and Other Wastes (Management and Transboundary Movement) Rules, 2016
  • The Bio-Medical Waste Management Rules, 2016
  • The Plastic Waste Management Rules, 2016
  • The E-Waste Management Rules, 2016
  • The Construction and Demolition Waste Management Rules, 2016

Recent Initiatives

In addition to its regulatory role, TNPCB has launched innovative initiatives to promote sustainability:

  • Implementing Zero Waste Management in urban areas.
  • Promoting the use of green technologies in industries.
  • Setting up air quality monitoring stations across major cities.
  • Conducting workshops and training sessions for environmental awareness.

Monitoring and Inspection

Industries are monitored based on pollution load, categorized as Red, Orange, or Green. Regular inspections and sample collections are conducted:

Type Category Inspection Frequency Sample Collection Frequency
Large Red Once in 3 months Once a month
Medium Orange Once in 6 months Once every 4 months
Small Green Once in 2 years Once in 6 months

Special Initiatives

Through focused programs, TNPCB is making strides in environmental preservation:

  • Facilitating Common Effluent Treatment Plants (CETPs) for small-scale industries.
  • Identifying safe sites for hazardous waste disposal.
  • Establishing the Environmental Training Institute to educate stakeholders.
